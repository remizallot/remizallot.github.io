While part of the [Enzyme Function Initiative](https://efi.igb.illinois.edu) team at the Carl R. Woese Institute for Genomic Biology, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, I helped develop and enhance the EFI-EST, EFI-GNT, and EFI-CGFP genomic enzymology web tools, used by researchers worldwide to discover novel enzyme functions and metabolic pathways. This work is described across three papers with Nils Oberg and John Gerlt:

- ["The EFI web resource for genomic enzymology tools: leveraging protein, genome, and metagenome databases to discover novel enzymes and metabolic pathways"](/publication/2019-01-01-The-EFI-web-resource-for-genomic-enzymology-tools-leveraging-protein-genome-and-metagenome-databases-to-discover-novel-enzymes-and-metabolic-pathways), *Biochemistry*, 2019
- ["Discovery of new enzymatic functions and metabolic pathways using genomic enzymology web tools"](/publication/2021-01-01-Discovery-of-new-enzymatic-functions-and-metabolic-pathways-using-genomic-enzymology-web-tools), *Current Opinion in Biotechnology*, 2021
- ["EFI-EST, EFI-GNT, and EFI-CGFP: enzyme function initiative (EFI) web resource for genomic enzymology tools"](/publication/2023-01-01-EFI-EST-EFI-GNT-and-EFI-CGFP-enzyme-function-initiative-EFI-web-resource-for-genomic-enzymology-tools), *Journal of Molecular Biology*, 2023
